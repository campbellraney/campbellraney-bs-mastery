SHIP’S LOG

A diary by Capt. Crit 'Red Beard' McGillicutty

Thar be monsters in the sea

Larry the Hairy

A wretched mass of sea-soaked fur and ancient rage, risen from the blackest depths. A foul spawn of salt and shadow, it drags ships to the deep with a hunger that knows no end. When the fog rolls thick and the sea goes quiet, pray ye don’t hear its bubbling growl.

Curly Twist n' Turn-ye

A devil of the deep—serpent-long, slick as oil, and mad with the dance of the drownin’. Its coils rise like black ropes from the brine, draggin’ men to watery graves with a grin full o’ teeth and madness. It don’t kill for hunger, no—it kills for the thrill of the twist.

Moe

They whisper of Moe—“When ye meet him, you exist no Moe.” A hulking horror wrapped in shadow and silence, Moe don’t roar nor thrash—he simply *ends*. One blink he ain’t there, the next, he’s all there is. No wreckage, no screams—just the cold hush of the sea reclaimin’ what’s hers.
