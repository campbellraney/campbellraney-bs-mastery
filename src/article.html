SHIP’S LOG

A diary by Capt. Crit 'Red Beard' McGillicutty

By Capt. McGillicutty

Island Breeze Tribune

It was a dark and stormy night

Aye, gather 'round, ye scallywags, and let ol' Captain Red Beard spin ye a yarn from the high seas. 'Twas a foggy morn off the coast o’ Tortuga when I first laid eyes on the cursed Isle of Whispers. The wind were still, and the crew muttered o’ ghostly voices ridin’ the mist. But I, bold as a shark in shallow waters, ordered the sails hoisted and steered us straight into the unknown, smellin' treasure like a bloodhound to gold.

We dropped anchor near a black-sanded cove, where strange symbols danced on the rocks like the Devil’s handwriting. Me first mate, Peg-Leg Pete, refused to step foot ashore, claimin' he saw a phantom with no eyes starin’ from the trees. But I pressed on with five brave lads, blades drawn and hearts poundin’. Deeper in the jungle we found it — a chest of emeralds brighter than Davy Jones’s locker. But mark me words, the second we touched it, the jungle roared and the ground near swallowed us whole!

We barely made it back to the ship, missin’ two men and gainin a few more white hairs under me hat. That chest now sits in me quarters, locked tight and hummin' with a power I dare not speak of. So if ever ye hear whispers on the wind, best ye sail the other way — else ye be part o’ the isle’s next tale. Arrr!
